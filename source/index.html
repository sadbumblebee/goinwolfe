How we fell in love:

Her Story

Daniel and I were friends for several months before he first asked me out—although to be technical about it, it was really the second time.

The first time, he invited me to a gallery opening and when I declined (I was on a backpacking trip with a friend), he enthusiastically asked if we could chat about my trip over drinks. I agreed, nervous but excited about the prospect of a date with Daniel. Unfortunately, it turned out ​he was just interested in being friends at that point.

We continued seeing each other around DC, and once we discovered our mutual love of cooking and hosting dinner parties, we saw each other more often. Over the next few months we shared many meals and bottles of wine, and finally, after I was thoroughly confused about the status of our friendship, he asked me to dinner on an official date. A year later we moved to California!

His Story

Woah woah woah. First of all, I couldn't tell if Dana was even interested in drinks when we met up! The ​real ​story is: out one night at a work happy hour, I found myself losing track of time chatting with Dana over cooking, politics, and ​life in DC when we were separated at the ends of a long table. ​​I was trying to engage in the conversations with those around me, but I looked across the table ​and heard Dana's ​charming laughter while her friends leaned in​ ​to ​hear the story​ she was telling​.

I wanted to be sitting with her. I was falling for Dana. Asking her out on a date then seemed to high stakes! I knew how I felt and I feared rejection. Thankfully, one fateful​ night I hosted a​ Pho ​dinner party and finally​ asked Dana to a proper night out.

Not too long after, holding her hand while we drove across the country, we knew we made the right decision.

Full Belly Farm

The Venue

Since Dana's days ​as an undergraduate ​studying at Berkeley​​, she's enjoyed the fresh fruit and veggies from Full Belly Farm. W​hen we moved to California, we started receiving a CSA from Full Belly together—and when we visited the farm for the first time and​ realized we could rent their barn, we knew it was just right for our wedding.

We'll be exchanging vows in the seclusion of the farm's walnut orchard. Then ​the party will begin back at​ the​ barn ​with drinks​, appetizers,​ and lawn games. The setting sun ​over​ the valley will play backdrop to a family style ​four course meal made with seasonal veggies. Late into an evening of dancing, we'll depart to camp under the stars.
